Experience the journey of Lord Rama in a magical way

Ramayana is one of the oldest and most revered epics in Indian literature, composed by the sage Valmiki. It narrates the life and adventures of Lord Rama, the prince of Ayodhya, his exile of fourteen years, the abduction of his wife Sita by the demon king Ravana, and his eventual victory over evil with the help of his loyal devotee Hanuman and his brother Lakshmana. The story is not just about the triumph of good over evil but also about duty, honor, love, and sacrifice.

Explore different khandas

Bala Kanda

Birth and childhood of Lord Rama, his education and marriage to Sita.

Ayodhya Kanda

Rama's exile to the forest, Bharat's love for his brother, and sacrifice.

Aranya Kanda

Life in the forest, encounter with Shurpanakha, and Sita's abduction by Ravana.

Kishkindha Kanda

Rama meets Hanuman, helps Sugriva regain his kingdom, and prepares for the battle.

Sundara Kanda

Hanuman's heroic journey to Lanka and his meeting with Sita.

Yuddha Kanda

The great battle between Rama and Ravana, victory of good over evil.

Why should we read this?

  • It teaches values like truthfulness, loyalty, courage, and righteousness.
  • Reading the Ramayana is believed to purify the mind and soul.
  • It inspires readers to follow the path of virtue and truth.
  • It is deeply ingrained in Indian culture, influencing art, music, dance, and literature.
  • Understanding Ramayana helps in appreciating the cultural heritage and traditions of South Asia.
